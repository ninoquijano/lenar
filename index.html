§ 02

Profile

— A Practice in Slow Ground —

I make places that remember the ground they sit on. Each commission begins on foot — pacing the site, listening to it — before any line is drawn or polygon rendered.

Trained at the University of the Philippines and seasoned across studios in Singapore and Melbourne, I have spent twelve years translating terrain into inhabitable poetry. My work moves between two registers — the slow, hand-drawn study of soil & planting; and the precise, photoreal language of 3D visualisation that lets clients walk through a garden before the first stone is laid.

I work across scales: from a courtyard the size of a tablecloth, to civic parks measured in hectares. Always with the same quiet question — what does this ground want to become?

§ 04

Process

  1. i.

    Walk the ground

    Every commission begins with a slow site-walk. Soil, prevailing wind, shade patterns, the sound of the place — all noted before a line is drawn.

  2. ii.

    Hand drawing

    Concept plans worked in graphite and ink. Iteration is fast at this stage; the drawing argues with itself before computers are invited in.

  3. iii.

    3D visualisation

    Terrain, planting and light modelled in Rhino, Lumion and Twinmotion — producing photoreal stills and walk-throughs that let clients inhabit the project before construction.

  4. iv.

    Construction stewardship

    On site through the build, weekly. A landscape only becomes itself once planted — and the architect should be present when it does.
